**TASK DEFINITION – D-DIMENSIONAL SPHERICAL CODE**

**Challenge:** Implement a Python class that generates a set of $n$ points on the surface of a unit hypersphere in $\mathbb{R}^d$ ($S^{d-1}$) such that the **maximum cosine similarity** (inner product) between any pair of distinct points is minimized.

**Specific Benchmark:** $n=600, d=11$.
**General Requirement:** The code must be valid for any natural number $n \ge 2$ and dimension $d \ge 2$.

**GEOMETRIC SPECIFICATIONS**
1.  **Points (Items):**
    *   A collection of $n$ points: $X = \{x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n\}$.
    *   Each point $x_i \in \mathbb{R}^d$.
2.  **Constraint Surface:**
    *   Unit $(d-1)$-sphere: $\|x_i\|_2 = 1$ for all $i=1 \dots n$.
    *   Points must lie strictly on the surface of the hypersphere.
3.  **Objective:**
    *   Minimize the maximum pairwise inner product.

**OBJECTIVE FUNCTION**
Minimize the coherence (maximum cosine similarity): $\mu(X)$.
$$ \mu(X) := \max_{1 \leq i < j \leq n} (x_i \cdot x_j) $$
*Goal:* Produce a spherical code with the smallest possible maximum inner product (i.e., points should be as spread out/orthogonal as possible).

**CONSTRAINTS**
1.  **Spherical Constraint:** Every returned point must satisfy $\|x_i\| \approx 1.0$ (within floating point tolerance).
2.  **Count:** Exactly $n$ points must be returned.
3.  **Dimensions:** Points must have exactly $d$ coordinates.
4.  **Real Values:** No `NaN` or `Inf` values allowed.

**TARGETED PERTURBATION & LANDSCAPE TRAVERSAL (CRITICAL)**
A highly competitive `perturb` method must navigate a notoriously difficult, non-convex energy landscape filled with shallow local minima and saddle points. However, high-quality spherical codes are extremely delicate: applying naive, global random noise will destroy the carefully packed sub-structures. Your perturbation must be **surgical, structure-preserving, and highly non-trivial.**

To achieve State-of-the-Art (SOTA) performance, your `perturb` method should act as an orchestrator for a portfolio of advanced heuristics. To inspire your design, consider combining approaches such as:
*   **Active-Set Targeting:** Identifying the specific subset of points acting as the "bottleneck" (the pairs realizing the maximum cosine) and applying localized, repelling vector fields or geometric reflections exclusively to them, leaving the well-packed majority intact.
*   **Surrogate Relaxations:** Temporarily morphing the objective function (e.g., evaluating the configuration under LogSumExp, Riesz $s$-energy, or $p$-norms with varying temperatures) to dissolve the current local minimum and allow the points to smoothly slide into a deeper basin.
*   **Manifold & Subspace Moves:** Exploiting the geometry of $S^{d-1}$ via localized topological twists, tangent-space momentum, or subset rotations that alter the configuration's state without blowing up the existing invariants.
*   **Intensity Orchestration:** Using the `intensity` parameter to intelligently scale from the micro-adjustments of bottleneck points to meso-scale structural shifts.
